Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about surplus travel and how TravelSurplus works.

What exactly is surplus travel?

+

Surplus travel refers to premium travel inventory that goes unsold — empty hotel rooms, unfilled airline seats, open cruise cabins, and available tour spots. Rather than letting this inventory go to waste, providers release it at significantly reduced prices. TravelSurplus finds, verifies, and scores these deals so you can book premium experiences at a fraction of the typical cost.

How is TravelSurplus different from other deal sites?

+

Most deal sites just show you a price. We show you the story behind the deal. Every listing includes a SurplusScore™ (1–100), a clear explanation of why the deal exists, the catches (if any), refund realities, and who the deal is best for. We never use fake urgency, countdown timers, or inflated discounts. Transparency is our entire model.

What is the SurplusScore™?

+

The SurplusScore™ is our proprietary scoring system that rates every deal from 1 to 100 based on eight factors: discount percentage vs. typical price, quality of experience, scarcity, seasonality value, location value, flexibility required, cancellation risk, and refund reality. A 25% discount on an exceptional property can score higher than 60% off something mediocre. The score keeps us honest.

Are these deals legitimate? What's the catch?

+

Every deal is real and comes from legitimate providers — hotels, airlines, cruise lines, and tour operators. If there's a catch (strict cancellation policy, limited dates, odd flight times), we tell you upfront in every listing. No hidden fees, no bait-and-switch. If we can't explain why a deal is surplus, we don't publish it.

Do I need to be flexible to use TravelSurplus?

+

Flexibility helps, but it's not always required. Some deals are date-specific (like 48-hour flash deals), while others have wider windows (shoulder season rates, new hotel openings). We clearly label how much flexibility each deal requires so you can filter for what works with your schedule. The more flexible you are, the more surplus you'll find.

How fast do surplus deals disappear?

+

It depends on the type. 48-hour surplus vanishes quickly. Midweek deals may last a few weeks. Shoulder-season surplus can be available for months. Each listing shows estimated availability and what typically sells out first. Sign up for alerts so you never miss a deal that matches your preferences.
